All Together

A summary of engineering class

In our engineering class, we were told to form groups and make a project that redesigned something in existence to run automatically.
We decided to build a greenhouse.
We focused on automating lighting, alarms and indicators, watering, security and access, and temperature control.
As the deadline got closer, we started staying after school on half days with our teachers to finish everything.
One day, we were working on a circuit, and something wasn’t right. The sensor wasn’t behaving the way it should. One of our teachers stepped in to fix it.
We moved away to work on the physical structure instead.
A few minutes passed.
Then we smelled smoke.
We all stopped talking and slowly turned around.
The component our teacher was working on had caught on fire.
We all made eye contact at the exact same time and immediately started laughing as our teacher frantically tried to put it out before the fire alarms went off.
The greenhouse survived and we went on to get extra credit on that project.
