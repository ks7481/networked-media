Friend B

A story with no ending...yet

We were at orientation for a dual enrollment program at a college.
At the end, we were told to go to the front of the lecture hall to pick up the textbook for our course.
We got in line for the calculus textbook.
As we moved forward in the line, we noticed that there were two options.
A red textbook and a blue one.
Naturally, this turned into a debate about which colors belonged to which subjects. By the time we reached the front of the line, we were fully invested.
The man handing out the books reached for the blue one.
My friend immediately protested.
“Could we get the red textbooks instead?”
He asked why.
My friend didn’t even hesitate.
“Obviously because math is red.”
He disagreed. He said math was blue.
This was unacceptable.
He gave us the red textbooks anyway, but the damage had been done. There was no way we were letting him say math was blue and just move on with our lives.
We went back and forth with him for a bit, but eventually we had to leave. We decided we would come back later and finish the argument properly.
It’s been three years.
We still haven’t had that argument.
