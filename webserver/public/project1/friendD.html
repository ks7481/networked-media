Friend D

The art class where no art was done

It was another Friday in our AP 2D Art and Design class.
The last period of the day.
As they did every class, our teachers reminded us how far we were supposed to be in our portfolios before leaving us to work.
But it was Friday.
And AP Art was last period.
We were not going to do any work.
The moment we were released, the talking started. Just enough to pretend we were being productive. About ten minutes later, the entire class was talking instead of working.
Getting fed up, our teachers told us to be silent and focus.
About eight minutes into that silence, the table next to us and my friend started whispering.
Then my friend leaned back in her chair and, in the most normal tone possible, said something so random and out of pocket that the room didn’t process it.
A few seconds passed.
The silence got heavier.
Then it hit everyone at once.
The entire room froze.
And then the laughter started.
My friend immediately dropped her head and covered her mouth, realizing far too late that the room had been way too quiet.
No one got any work done after that.
